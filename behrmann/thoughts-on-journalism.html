Thoughts on Journalism

  1. You’re paid to break news, not to type. 
  2. It doesn’t matter how many questions you ask. What matters is how much information you receive.
  3. If you can’t meet deadline and can’t write to length, you’re not a reporter. 
  4. Sourcing + reading = ideas
  5. If you can’t explain why the story matters, you don’t have a story. 
  6. Data without color is a spreadsheet; color without data is a campfire story. 
  7. The secret to success: Call one more source, ask one more question, write one more story. 
  8. If you’re not telling readers something that someone doesn’t want them to know, you’re not going deep enough.
  9. Source for emergencies. 
  10. No great story was ever found in the office. 
  11. There’s nothing wrong with B1 stories — but aim to write them onto A1. 
  12. The value of a story is its impact, not its length. 
  13. If you’re only using words to tell your story, you’re not trying hard enough. 
  14. Nach dem Spiel ist vor dem Spiel.
