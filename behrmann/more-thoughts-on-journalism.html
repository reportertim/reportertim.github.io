More Thoughts on Journalism

  1. If you don’t know who you’re writing for, you can’t write a good story.
  2. Every opportunity to ask a question should be seized.
  3. The only way to be a successful beat reporter is to juggle multiple stories.
  4. Having correct style isn’t the most important thing – but it’s a very important thing.
  5. Having voice is important, but it is secondary to reporting.
  6. If you can’t come up with meaningful things to write about, you’re not a reporter.
  7. Good stories are actionable.
  8. If you don’t understand something, it shouldn’t be in your story.
  9. Most stories have too many quotes.
  10. Ambitious work builds on basic work.
  11. All good journalism comes from good sourcing.
  12. If you’re not enjoying this, you shouldn’t be doing it.
