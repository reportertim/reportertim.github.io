First Coast Distillery Data Timothy Gibbons/Jacksonville Business Journal 2024-05-25

Overview

As of May 25, 2024, there are 118 distilleries with current licenses in the state of Florida, dating back to Drum Circle Distilling, which received its license on August 27, 2008.

This data updates automatically when the script is run, but it can be manually downloaded here.

On the First Coast1, there are 17 distilleries with current licenses. The first, Marlin & Barrel Distillery Llc, was issued its license on an original date of August 21, 2014.

An embeddable version of this chart can be found here.

Growth

The number of distilleries across the state has been steadily rising. The most recent distillery to be licensed in Florida is Channelside Brewing Company in Tampa, which was permitted on May 24, 2024.

On the First Coast, the most recent permit was issued to Jekyll Brewing in Jacksonville Beach on an effective date of May 15, 2024.

Heres how the distillery count across the First Coast has been increasing over the years. Note that this only includes distilleries that still have an active license.

Sales

The state collects data (for taxation purposes) on direct sales by distilleries generally speaking, these are sales made to tourists visiting the distillery. (Unfortunately, overall sales numbers, showing sales through the distiller-wholesaler-retailer system, are not available.)

This data must be downloaded manually; the most recent information was pulled May 25, 2024. The data can be found here.

Distilleries across the state obviously saw those direct sales collapse during the height of the Covid-19 pandemic. These charts show both how theyve rebounded and the effects of legal changes regarding how much can be sold.

Embeddable versions of the Florida chart is here and of the First Coast chart is here

The distillery in Florida that has sold the most liquor directly to consumers so far in 2023 is City Gates Beverage Company, which has sold 13,918.49 gallons.

On the First Coast, City Gates Beverage Companys taproom moved the largest amount of liquor, selling 13,918.49 gallons directly to consumers.

In the latest 12 months of data from April 2023 to March 2024 the 15 distilleries in the area that reported sales moved 33,002.22 gallons of liquor.
